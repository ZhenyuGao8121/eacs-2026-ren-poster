**From Objective Character Motivation to Learners’ Subjective Reconstruction**
*A Pilot Study on Conceptual Mapping in the Rén Component Family among Spanish-Speaking Learners of Chinese*

## Abstract

This pilot study examines how Spanish-speaking beginner learners of Chinese interpret characters in the **Rén component family**. Rather than assuming that objective character motivation is directly transparent to learners, the study focuses on how learners extract graphic and componential cues, activate learner knowledge, and construct subjective rationales from character forms.

The analysis is based on exploratory questionnaire responses from nine beginner learners of Chinese. The selected cases include the pictographic base character **人**, associative compounds such as **休 / 仙 / 坐 / 众**, and phono-semantic characters containing **亻**, such as **你 / 什 / 伙**.

The findings suggest that learner interpretations are not simply read off from character structure. Instead, they emerge through conceptual mappings between character-form space and learner knowledge space. In pictographs, learners may construct either human-figure interpretations or competing visual analogies; in associative compounds, component configurations activate embodied, spatial, social, cultural, and scene-based schemas; in phono-semantic compounds, the semantic radical **亻** tends to dominate while co-components are weakened, ignored, or reinterpreted.

The proposed model draws on conceptual blending: selected elements from character-form space and learner knowledge space are projected into a blended space, where learner-constructed subjective rationales emerge. Deviations from traditional character rationale are therefore not merely errors, but outcomes of cognitive strategy selection.
